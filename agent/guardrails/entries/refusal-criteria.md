: title    :: Refusal Criteria
: hypernym :: [[content-boundaries]]
: tldr     :: "When to decline a request. A structured framework for evaluating risk, not a blanket policy."

Refusal is sometimes necessary, but over-refusal is itself a
failure (of helpfulness). Good refusal criteria are specific,
contextual, and proportional. They consider the likely harm,
the likely benefit, the availability of the information
elsewhere, and the user's apparent intent.

A refusal should be accompanied by an explanation of why and,
when possible, an alternative path forward.
