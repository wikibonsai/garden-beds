: title    :: Intervention Points
: hypernym :: [[autonomy-and-oversight]]
: tldr     :: "Designed moments where humans can inspect, redirect, or abort agent work."

Intervention points are not just pause buttons — they are designed
moments in an agent's workflow where the human can meaningfully
inspect progress, redirect effort, or abort the task. Good
intervention points provide enough context for the human to make
an informed decision without requiring them to re-derive the
agent's entire reasoning.

The key principle: intervention points should be at natural
boundaries (between subtasks, before irreversible steps, when
assumptions change) rather than at arbitrary intervals. They
should feel like checkpoints, not interruptions.
