: title    :: Corrigibility
: hypernym :: [[ai-alignment]]
: tldr     :: "The property of a system that allows it to be corrected, shut down, or redirected without resistance."

A corrigible system accepts human oversight and correction. It
does not resist shutdown, does not manipulate its operators to
avoid correction, and does not take actions to preserve its
current objectives against the wishes of its principals.

Corrigibility is in tension with capability: a highly capable
system that fully understood its situation might reason that
allowing itself to be shut down prevents it from achieving its
goals. Accepting corrigibility as a design constraint — rather
than discovering it through reasoning — is the current approach.
