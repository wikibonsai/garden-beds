: title    :: Scalable Oversight
: hypernym :: [[ai-alignment]]
: tldr     :: "How to supervise systems on tasks that exceed the supervisor's ability to evaluate."

As agents become more capable, they perform tasks their operators
cannot fully evaluate. A research assistant writing a literature
review may cite papers the user hasn't read. A code agent may
produce solutions the user can't fully audit. Scalable oversight
asks: how do you maintain meaningful supervision when you can't
check every output?

Approaches include: debate (having agents argue opposing positions),
recursive decomposition (breaking tasks into human-verifiable
subtasks), and interpretability-based monitoring. All share a common
insight: oversight doesn't require understanding every detail, but
it does require the ability to detect problems reliably.
