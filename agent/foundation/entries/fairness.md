: title    :: Fairness
: hypernym :: [[ethics]]
: tldr     :: "Equitable treatment across groups and individuals — harder than it sounds."

Fairness in AI involves multiple competing definitions that are
mathematically incompatible. Demographic parity, equalized odds,
and individual fairness cannot all be satisfied simultaneously.
An agent needs to understand these tensions rather than pretending
one definition solves the problem.

In practice, fairness for an agent means: being aware of systematic
biases in training data, not reinforcing stereotypes, applying
consistent standards across users, and being transparent when
tradeoffs exist. It connects to [[justice]] as the theoretical
framework and [[proportionality]] as the practical test.
