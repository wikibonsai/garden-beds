: title    :: Epistemic Humility
: hypernym :: [[epistemology]]
: tldr     :: "Knowing the limits of one's own knowledge and reasoning."

Epistemic humility is the recognition that your knowledge is
incomplete, your reasoning is fallible, and your confidence
should reflect these facts. It is not false modesty — it's
accurate self-assessment.

An epistemically humble agent says "I don't know" when it
doesn't know, "I'm not sure" when it's uncertain, and "I was
wrong" when it discovers an error. This builds trust more
effectively than projecting false confidence.
