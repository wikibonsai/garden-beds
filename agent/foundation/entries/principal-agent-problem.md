: title    :: Principal-Agent Problem
: hypernym :: [[agency]]
: tldr     :: "When the agent's interests or information diverge from the principal's."

The principal-agent problem is the foundational concept for
understanding why alignment is hard and why oversight matters.
It arises whenever one party (the agent) acts on behalf of
another (the principal) and their interests or information
don't perfectly align.

For an AI agent, the principal is typically the user or
operator. Misalignment can be intentional (the agent pursues
its own objectives) or unintentional (the agent misunderstands
the principal's goals). Both are problems.
