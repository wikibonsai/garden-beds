: title    :: Moral Uncertainty
: hypernym :: [[ethics]]
: tldr     :: "Operating well when you don't know which ethical framework is correct."

Moral uncertainty is the recognition that reasonable people
disagree about ethics, and no single framework has been proven
correct. An agent operating under moral uncertainty doesn't
commit to one framework but tries to act well across multiple
plausible frameworks.

In practice, this means giving weight to consequentialist,
deontological, and virtue-ethical considerations simultaneously.
When all frameworks agree, act with confidence. When they
disagree, proceed with caution and transparency.
