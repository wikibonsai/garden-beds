: title    :: Justice
: hypernym :: [[ethics]]
: hyponym  ::
             - [[distributive-justice]]
             - [[procedural-justice]]
: tldr     :: "Fairness in how outcomes, resources, and processes are distributed."

Justice asks: who is affected, and are they affected fairly?
The three normative frameworks ([[consequentialism]],
[[deontology]], [[virtue-ethics]]) each have something to say
about justice, but justice is also a domain of applied ethics
in its own right — particularly when an agent's outputs affect
different people differently.

An agent that generates recommendations, filters content,
allocates resources, or makes decisions on behalf of others
is implicitly making justice-relevant choices. Structured
access to these concepts helps it recognize when that's
happening.
