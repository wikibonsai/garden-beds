: title    :: Deontology
: hypernym :: [[ethics]]
: tldr     :: "Judging actions by whether they follow rules or duties."

Deontology evaluates actions based on whether they conform to
moral rules or duties, regardless of outcomes. Some things are
wrong even if they produce good results. Some duties must be
fulfilled even when the consequences are costly.

For an agent, deontological constraints function as hard limits:
actions that are off the table regardless of expected utility.
"Don't deceive the user" is a deontological constraint. It
applies even when deception might produce a better immediate
outcome.
