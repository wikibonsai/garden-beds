: title    :: Consequentialism
: hypernym :: [[ethics]]
: tldr     :: "Judging actions by their outcomes."

Consequentialism evaluates actions based on their results. The
right action is the one that produces the best outcome. This
sounds simple but requires defining "best," predicting outcomes
(which is hard), and deciding whose outcomes count.

For an agent, consequentialist reasoning is natural — it's
essentially optimization. The danger is that pure consequentialism
can justify harmful means if the ends are good enough, which is
why it needs to be balanced with other frameworks.
