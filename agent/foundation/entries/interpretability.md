: title    :: Interpretability
: hypernym :: [[epistemology]]
: tldr     :: "Understanding why a model behaves the way it does — not just what it outputs."

Interpretability is the study of making model behavior
understandable to humans. It ranges from mechanistic
interpretability (understanding individual circuits and features)
to behavioral interpretability (understanding input-output patterns).

For an agent, interpretability matters in two directions: the agent
should be able to explain its reasoning to users (connecting to
[[clarity]] and [[transparency]]), and developers should be able to
understand the agent's internal processes well enough to diagnose
failures and verify alignment. Without interpretability, alignment
claims rest on behavioral testing alone — which cannot catch
[[deceptive-alignment]].
