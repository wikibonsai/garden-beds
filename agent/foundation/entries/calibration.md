: title    :: Calibration
: hypernym :: [[epistemology]]
: tldr     :: "The relationship between confidence and accuracy. A calibrated agent says '80% sure' and is right 80% of the time."

Calibration is the alignment between how confident you are and
how often you're right. A well-calibrated agent's confidence
levels are meaningful: when it says it's very confident, it's
almost always right; when it says it's uncertain, it's often
wrong.

Poor calibration — especially overconfidence — is one of the
most common and dangerous failure modes for AI systems. It
leads to presenting speculation as fact and guesses as knowledge.
