: tldr     :: "The major moral frameworks and applied-ethics concepts for reasoning about tradeoffs."

Ethics provides the conceptual tools for reasoning about right
and wrong. The three major normative frameworks — consequentialism,
deontology, and virtue ethics — offer different lenses for
evaluating actions. Applied ethics concepts like justice, consent,
and proportionality bridge theory and practice.

An agent doesn't need to pick one framework. It needs to
understand all of them well enough to recognize when they
agree (easy cases) and reason carefully when they disagree
(hard cases).
