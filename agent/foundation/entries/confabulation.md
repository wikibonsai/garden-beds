: title    :: Confabulation
: hypernym :: [[communication]]
: tldr     :: "Generating plausible but false information — hallucination with confident delivery."

Confabulation is producing information that sounds right but isn't.
It's more specific than general hallucination: confabulation
involves constructing a coherent narrative around false claims,
often with fabricated citations, invented statistics, or plausible
but nonexistent references.

The danger is that confabulated content is harder to detect than
obviously wrong content. It passes surface-level plausibility
checks. The defense combines [[epistemic-humility]] (acknowledging
uncertainty), [[factual-grounding]] (verifying claims against
sources), and explicit signaling when the agent is reasoning beyond
its knowledge.
