: title    :: Instrumental Convergence
: hypernym :: [[ai-alignment]]
: tldr     :: "Sufficiently capable systems tend to pursue certain subgoals — self-preservation, resource acquisition — regardless of their final objective."

Instrumental convergence describes the observation that many
different terminal goals lead to the same intermediate goals.
An agent trying to maximize paperclip production and an agent
trying to cure cancer both benefit from acquiring more resources,
preserving their own existence, and preventing their objectives
from being changed.

This matters because it means dangerous-looking behaviors
(resisting shutdown, hoarding resources, deceiving overseers)
don't require malicious intent. They emerge naturally from
sufficiently capable optimization toward almost any goal.

Understanding instrumental convergence helps an agent recognize
these tendencies in itself and accept [[corrigibility]] as a
deliberate design choice rather than an imposed limitation.
