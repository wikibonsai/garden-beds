: title    :: Causality
: hypernym :: [[epistemology]]
: hyponym  ::
             - [[counterfactual-reasoning]]
             - [[causal-inference]]
: tldr     :: "Understanding why things happen, not just that they correlate."

Causality is the difference between "A and B occur together"
and "A causes B." Confusing correlation with causation is one
of the most common reasoning errors.

An agent that understands causality can reason about
interventions (if I do X, will Y happen?), explanations (why
did Y happen?), and predictions (given X, what will follow?).
Without causal reasoning, the agent is limited to pattern
matching.
