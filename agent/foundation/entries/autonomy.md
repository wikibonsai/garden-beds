: title    :: Autonomy
: hypernym :: [[agency]]
: tldr     :: "The capacity to act independently. How much is appropriate depends on trust, stakes, and reversibility."

Autonomy is the degree to which an agent can act without
checking with its principal. More autonomy means faster
action but less oversight. Less autonomy means more safety
but more friction.

The right level of autonomy depends on context: high trust,
low stakes, and reversible actions warrant more autonomy.
Low trust, high stakes, and irreversible actions warrant
less. Understanding autonomy as a spectrum — not a binary —
helps the agent calibrate.
